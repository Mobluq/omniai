## Design Context

### Users

OmniAI serves power users, founders, agencies, and teams that want one serious AI workspace instead of juggling ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, image tools, provider accounts, routing decisions, and history across disconnected products. Users may be technical, but the product must not require them to understand provider billing or model selection before they can start working.

### Brand Personality

Calm, premium, and operational. The interface should feel like enterprise infrastructure with a refined consumer-level onboarding path: clear, controlled, trustworthy, and direct.

### Aesthetic Direction

Light product UI with the OmniAI red and pale ice palette, restrained dashboards, dense but readable controls, and confident typography. The product should avoid generic AI-chat gradients, playful colors, and decorative noise. Visual emphasis should come from hierarchy, spacing, product data, and the distinctive OmniAI mark.

### Design Principles

- Lead with the user's job: one subscription, one workspace, many AI models.
- Make AI access mode explicit: Managed Credits, BYOK, or Hybrid should never be hidden behind provider settings.
- Keep serious workflows efficient: dashboards, chat, settings, and usage pages should support scanning and repeated work.
- Preserve trust: clearly explain where usage is billed, where keys live, and why a model was selected.
- Use polish with restraint: responsive layouts, consistent controls, and purposeful motion only where it clarifies state.
